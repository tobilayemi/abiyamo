The Problem

Maternal Deaths That Are Largely Preventable

Nigeria accounts for nearly 12% of global maternal deaths, despite comprising only 2.4% of the world's population. The majority of these deaths happen in primary and secondary health facilities where clinical tools are scarce and workload is high.

Frontline health workers often lack structured decision support for identifying high-risk pregnancies during routine ANC visits — leading to delayed referrals, missed danger signs, and preventable deaths.

Abiyamo addresses this by bringing a hybrid AI risk engine directly to the point of care, in the hands of any clinician with a browser.

Risk Categories

Four Levels, Clear Clinical Meaning

Every assessment resolves to one of four risk categories, each mapped to a set of immediate actions and a referral decision.

Level 4
Critical

Life-threatening condition requiring emergency intervention. Examples: severe pre-eclampsia, severe anaemia (Hb < 7), antepartum haemorrhage.

Emergency Referral
Level 3
High

Significant risk needing urgent clinical action within 24 hours. Examples: pre-eclampsia, moderate-severe anaemia, malaria in pregnancy, HIV-PMTCT.

Urgent Action
Level 2
Moderate

Elevated risk requiring enhanced monitoring and management. Examples: gestational hypertension, mild anaemia, obesity, previous caesarean section.

Enhanced Monitoring
Level 1
Low

No significant risk factors identified. Routine ANC care recommended with standard supplementation, immunisation, and birth preparedness counselling.
